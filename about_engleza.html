Our history

Stamp Factory — since 1872, heritage printing for Romania

The history of the Stamp Factory begins on February 29, 1872, with the Stamp and Registration Law. In the same year, within the Ministry of Finance, the Service for the Manufacture, Conservation and Distribution of Stamps was founded — the core of the current Factory. The first equipment were four hand presses brought from Vienna, and starting in 1876 we printed postage stamps based on galvanic clichés made in Paris. Since then, we have printed fiscal and postage stamps, engraved sheets, policies, money orders, postcards (domestic since 1873, international since 1879) and many other items with security features.

Founded in 1872; uninterrupted tradition
Fiscal & postage stamps, money orders, postcards
Three printing technologies: relief, planographic, intaglio
Anti-counterfeit: microtext, “FT” watermark, special inks, holograms

From 1907 we went through successive modernization waves; in 1911 one of the most appreciated fiscal stamp issues (King Carol I) appeared, and from 1927 we introduced advanced protection techniques. Today we are a unique printing house in Romania: we operate all three printing types, we produce offset polychromy (catalogues, posters, labels, stickers, calendars, books, magazines) as well as envelope personalization, perforation and numbering. Recent upgrades (high-speed digital printing, inserting line, integrated workflows) allow us to deliver fast, safely and with full traceability. Our mission is to remain the preferred supplier of printing products and services through quality, promptness and solutions tailored to each client.

Sebastian Burduja

Minister of Energy
“Romanian Post is modernizing — an important message that shows how vital the stability of a leadership team is. We are talking about a team that has delivered, even though it is not on a full mandate. I very much want us to have stability and predictability. The difference between a politician and a leader is simple: politicians think about the next elections, leaders think about the next generations. What we see today, here, is a team and a company that thinks about the next generations, looks to and respects the past — a 160-year history — but also has a forward-looking perspective.”

Valentin Ștefan

Director General, Romanian Post
“In the year when the Stamp Factory celebrates 150 years of activity and Romanian Post marks 160 years, we can see many results of the company’s transformation. The equipment brought in by Romanian Post is the key to developing the Stamp Factory in the medium and long term. It is another step toward what we aim to achieve at Romanian Post: digitalization, revitalization and performance. In simple terms, from today Romanian Post can participate in major tenders and contracts without having to become a subcontractor or partner, being able to take on integrated jobs of printing, inserting and dispatch.”
